Tennis Rules

Tennis is a sport that is played on a rectangular court by either two players (singles) or four players (doubles). The objective of the game is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent's court in a way that it cannot be returned. Points are scored when the opponent fails to return the ball within the prescribed dimensions of the court.

There are various rules governing aspects such as serving, scoring, and player conduct, which are enforced by trained officials such as umpires and line judges.

Grand Slam Schedule

The Grand Slam tournaments are the most prestigious events in tennis, consisting of four major championships held annually:

  • Australian Open - usually held in January in Melbourne, Australia
  • French Open (Roland Garros) - usually held in May and June in Paris, France
  • Wimbledon - usually held in June and July in London, England
  • US Open - usually held in August and September in New York City, USA
